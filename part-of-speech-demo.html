📋 Available Parts of Speech

Traditional Parts of Speech:
  • Noun: Person, place, thing, or idea (e.g., "book", "happiness")
  • Verb: Action or state of being (e.g., "run", "is")
  • Adjective: Describes a noun (e.g., "beautiful", "tall")
  • Adverb: Describes a verb, adjective, or other adverb (e.g., "quickly", "very")
  • Pronoun: Replaces a noun (e.g., "he", "she", "it")
  • Preposition: Shows relationship (e.g., "in", "on", "at")
  • Conjunction: Connects words or phrases (e.g., "and", "but", "or")
  • Interjection: Expresses emotion (e.g., "wow", "ouch")
Special Categories:
  • Article: Definite or indefinite articles (e.g., "the", "a", "an")
  • Determiner: Words that introduce nouns (e.g., "this", "that", "some")
  • Numeral: Numbers (e.g., "one", "first", "hundred")
  • Auxiliary Verb: Helping verbs (e.g., "be", "have", "do")
  • Modal Verb: Expresses possibility, necessity, etc. (e.g., "can", "must", "should")
  • Phrasal Verb: Verb + preposition/adverb (e.g., "look up", "give in")
  • Idiom: Figurative expression (e.g., "break a leg", "piece of cake")
  • Expression: Common phrases or sayings
